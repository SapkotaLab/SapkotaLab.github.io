The lab develops risk models that help stratify childhood cancer survivors by their likelihood of later disease.

Those models combine clinical variables with genomic information so clinicians can choose the level of screening that fits a survivor's risk profile.

Representative outcomes include cardiomyopathy, severe obesity, hypertension, and type 2 diabetes.
